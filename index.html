The Blueprint

⸻ ✦ ⸻

Three consecutive verses of scripture — each containing exactly seventy-two Hebrew consonants. Read in alternating directions, they yield the seventy-two divine Names constituting the Shemhamphorash: the 216-letter Name of God.

The Three Verses of Exodus 14

Kabbalists of late antiquity discovered that Exodus 14:19, 20, and 21 each contain exactly 72 consonants — a perfection unique in all of scripture. This was understood not as coincidence but as divine cipher: the Name of God hidden within the account of the parting of the Red Sea, the supreme moment of divine intervention in Israel's history.

The Boustrophedon Method

Boustrophedon (βουστροφηδόν — "as the ox plows") is an ancient alternating-direction cipher. For each position n from 1 to 72: take letter n of Verse 19 (left to right), letter 73−n of Verse 20 (right to left, reversed), and letter n of Verse 21 (left to right). Stack the three letters — and the Name is revealed.

The Theophoric Suffixes

The 72 raw triplets are extended with either -יה (Yah, contracted Tetragrammaton) or -אל (El, "God") to form pronounceable angelic names. Both affirm that each Name is a face of the One. The bare triplets are used in advanced rites; the suffixed forms in prayer and invocation.

The Number 216

Three verses × 72 letters = 216 letters total. 216 = 6³, and 6 is the value of Vav (ו), the connecting letter. The sum of the letters of Gevurah (גבורה, "divine strength") also equals 216. Each triplet carries all three modes of divine action simultaneously — the Angel's providence, the cloud's discernment, and Moses' will — a triad within a triad encoding the complete mechanism of divine power in the world.

Computational verification: applying the boustrophedon algorithm to the vowel-stripped Masoretic text of Exodus 14:19–21 reproduces all 72 traditional triplets. Nine positions produce final (sofit) letter forms; these are normalised to standard forms in the traditional name list — a scribal convention, not a mathematical error.

אנא בכח

⸸ Ana Bekoach · The 42-Letter Name of God · Prayer of Power ⸸

The Ana Bekoach — "Please, with the strength of your right hand's greatness, untie the bundled sins" — is among the most potent prayers in Kabbalistic tradition. Forty-two words across seven lines encode the 42-Letter Name of God, derived by taking the initial letter of each word. Its power is described in the Talmud (Kiddushin 71a) as one of the great secret names transmitted only to the most worthy disciples. Each line invokes one of the seven lower Sephiroth, channelling divine light from Atziluth down through successive grades to Malkhut.

The Lord's Prayer & the Tree of Life

⸸ ✟ ⸸

The Lord's Prayer — the central prayer of Christian devotion, taught by Jesus in the Sermon on the Mount — is, from the perspective of Christian Kabbalah, a systematic descent through the ten Sephiroth of the Tree of Life: a complete theurgical circuit from the Crown of divine transcendence to the Kingdom of material redemption, spoken in ten phrases.

The Tree of Life as Structure of Prayer

Christian Kabbalah — a tradition developed from the 15th century onwards by scholars such as Pico della Mirandola, Johannes Reuchlin, and later the Rosicrucians and Golden Dawn — recognised that the Ten Sephiroth provide the hidden architecture of Christian prayer. The Lord's Prayer, with its ten clauses (nine petitions plus the implied doxology), maps precisely onto the ten divine emanations, revealing the prayer as a full Kabbalistic working: a deliberate passage of consciousness from Kether (the Father) down the Tree to Malkuth (the Kingdom), and an invocation of divine energy into the material world.

The Doxology

"For Thine is the Kingdom, the Power, and the Glory, for ever and ever. Amen."

For Thine is the Kingdom (Malkuth), the Power (Geburah), and the Glory (Hod), for ever and ever, Amen. — The doxology names three Sephiroth directly: Kingdom (מלכות), Power (גבורה), and Glory (הוד) — affirming in a single breath the base, the left pillar of severity, and the reflection of divine splendour.

On Christian Kabbalah

The tradition of Christian Kabbalah holds that Kabbalah illuminates the deepest meanings of Christian doctrine. Giovanni Pico della Mirandola (1463–1494) argued in his 900 Theses that Kabbalistic analysis proves the divinity of Christ more effectively than any purely scriptural argument. His contemporary Johannes Reuchlin wrote De Arte Cabalistica (1517), the first systematic Christian Kabbalistic treatise. The Rosicrucian manifests of the early 17th century, the work of Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa, and the entire tradition of the Hermetic Kabbalah synthesised Jewish, Christian, Neoplatonic, and Egyptian sources into a unified esoteric system — of which this Wheel is one expression.

From this perspective, the Lord's Prayer is not merely a devotional text but a complete cosmological map: beginning at the apex of the Tree in pure divine transcendence ("Our Father in Heaven") and descending through all ten Sephiroth until the divine reaches the physical world ("Deliver us from evil" — the redemption of Malkuth). To pray the Lord's Prayer with Kabbalistic awareness is to consciously traverse the entire Tree of Life with every recitation.

Sepher Yetzirah

⸻ ✡ ⸻

The Sepher Yetzirah (ספר יצירה, "Book of Formation"), attributed to the patriarch Abraham and composed between the 3rd and 6th centuries CE, is the earliest extant text of speculative Jewish Kabbalah. It describes God's creation of the universe through 32 Paths of Wisdom: the 10 Sephiroth and the 22 Hebrew letters — the same 22 letters that combine in the boustrophedon of Exodus 14 to yield the 72 Names.

Part I — The 231 Gates: All Hebrew Letters in Combination

The 231 Gates — every pair of Hebrew letters connected, rendered as a geometric mandala

In Chapter 2 of the Sepher Yetzirah, God arranges the 22 Hebrew letters in a circle — like a wall with gates — and connects every letter to every other. The text declares:

"Twenty-two Foundation Letters. He placed them in a circle like a wall with 231 Gates. The circle oscillates back and forth... Two stones build 2 houses. Three stones build 6 houses. Four stones build 24 houses. Five stones build 120 houses. Six stones build 720 houses. From here onward go out and think what the mouth cannot speak and the ear cannot hear."

— Sepher Yetzirah, Chapter 2 (Gra recension)

The Mathematics of the 231 Gates

The number 231 arises from a simple combinatorial principle: if you arrange 22 objects in a circle and draw a line between every possible pair, how many lines do you draw?

Combinations of 22 letters taken 2 at a time:
C(22, 2) = 22 × 21 ÷ 2 = 231
Each pair is a Gate. 231 Gates = all possible two-letter roots of creation.

The mandala above renders this geometrically: 22 points placed on a circle (one for each Hebrew letter, labelled around the outer ring), with every point connected to every other by a straight line. The result is 231 chords — a dense, shimmering geometric pattern that the Kabbalists understood as the template of creation itself: the full set of "syllables" from which all things were spoken into being.

The Sepher Yetzirah divides these 22 letters into three groups: 3 Mothers (Alef, Mem, Shin — generating Air, Water, Fire and the three seasons), 7 Doubles (Bet through Tav — governing the seven classical planets, seven days of the week, and seven orifices of the body), and 12 Simples (Heh through Qof — corresponding to the twelve zodiac signs, twelve months, and twelve organs of action and perception).

The 231 Gates are formed by combining every letter with every other — both forward and backward. The forward combinations (Alef-Bet, Alef-Gimel, Alef-Dalet...) represent the positive, creative force; the reverse combinations (Bet-Alef, Gimel-Alef, Dalet-Alef...) represent the negative, receptive force. Together they constitute the entire alphabet of creation — every sound-frequency from which the universe is continuously spoken into existence.

Part II — The Cube of Space

The Cube of Space — 22 Hebrew letters assigned to the 32 elements of three-dimensional geometry

In Chapters 4–6 of the Sepher Yetzirah, the same 22 letters are mapped onto the geometry of three-dimensional space, forming the Cube of Space — a complete cosmological model in which every direction, axis, face, and edge of the spatial cube is named by a Hebrew letter.

The Three Axes — Mother Letters

  • Alef (א) — the North-South axis (Left ↔ Right). Air. The breath that moves horizontally through the world.
  • Mem (מ) — the East-West axis (Fore ↔ Back). Water. The deep horizontal axis of depth and the unconscious.
  • Shin (ש) — the vertical axis (Up ↔ Down). Fire. The axis of spiritual ascent and descent, Heaven and Earth.

The Six Faces — Double Letters

  • Bet (ב) — Above (the face of Heaven). Gimel (ג) — Below (the face of Earth).
  • Dalet (ד) — East. Kaf (כ) — West. Peh (פ) — North. Resh (ר) — South.
  • The 7th Double, Tav (ת), governs the Palace of Holiness at the Centre — the sanctum within all directions.

The Twelve Edges — Simple Letters

The 12 edges of the cube correspond to the 12 Simple letters — the same letters that govern the 12 signs of the zodiac and the 12 months of the year. Each edge connects two faces; each Simple letter mediates between two directions, two seasons, two tribes, two organs of the body.

  • Heh (ה) — NE above · Vav (ו) — SE above · Zayin (ז) — NE above-east corner (Aries–Taurus boundary)
  • Chet (ח) — NW above · Tet (ט) — SW above · Yod (י) — SE below
  • Lamed (ל) — NW edge · Nun (נ) — SW edge · Samekh (ס) — NE lower
  • Ayin (ע) — SW lower · Tzadi (צ) — SE lower · Qof (ק) — SW lower-west

The Cosmic Significance

The Cube of Space is the Sepher Yetzirah's answer to the question: "What is the shape of the universe?" The answer is a perfect cube, inscribed with the 22 Hebrew letters — the same letters that form the 231 Gates, that form the names of the 72 Angels, that form every word in scripture. Space itself is language; creation is a perpetual act of divine speech articulated in all three dimensions simultaneously.

In the Western Hermetic tradition, Paul Foster Case and the B.O.T.A. school further mapped the 22 Major Arcana of the Tarot onto the Cube of Space, completing a synthesis in which the geometric structure of three-dimensional reality, the 22 Hebrew letters, and the 22 Trump cards of the Tarot all describe the same underlying architecture of consciousness and cosmos.

The Wheel of the Shem ha-Mephorash and the Cube of Space are complementary maps: the Wheel unfolds the 22 letters across the circular ecliptic plane, yielding 72 Names; the Cube arranges the same 22 letters in three-dimensional space, yielding the full geometry of existence. Together they constitute the complete Kabbalistic cosmology — the flat map and the three-dimensional model of the same divine territory.
